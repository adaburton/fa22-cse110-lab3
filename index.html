Materialist Perspective

Read

The materialist faction has argued heavily that we can only look at how cereal functions materially in the life of the working class. We can see that soups and salads are both generally considered lunch or dinner foods, while cereal has always been a breakfast food, and therefore we cannot place cereal in either category and must categorize it as either a breakfast soup or breakfast salad. Thus the true question is whether cereal ought to be considered breakfast soup or breakfast salad. The faction argues that because the ratio of sauce to solid is closer to that of a soup, rather than a salad, the only conclusion a rational thinker can come to is that cereal is just another label for a class of foods that can be defined as a "breakfast soup".
